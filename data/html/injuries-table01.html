2 May include volunteers and workers receiving other types of compensation.

4 Persons identified as Hispanic or Latino may be of any race. The race categories shown exclude data for Hispanics and Latinos.

7 Based on the BLS Occupational Injury and Illness Classification System (OIICS) in effect for 1992 to 2010 data. The primary source of injury identifies the object,              
substance, or exposure that directly produced or inflicted the injury. For most transportation incidents, the primary source identifies the vehicle in which the              
 deceased was an occupant. For most falls, the primary source identifies the surface or object contacted.              
8 The category "Robber" was instituted beginning with 1997 data, which was partial year reporting.              
9 Based on the BLS Occupational Injury and Illness Classification System (OIICS) in effect for 1992 to 2010 data. The secondary source of injury, if any, identifies the              
object, substance, or person that generated the source of injury or that contributed to the event or exposure. For vehicle collisions, the deceased’s vehicle is the              
primary source and the other object (truck, road divider, etc.) is the secondary source. For most homicides, the "bullet" is the primary source and the "assailant" is              
the secondary source. For most falls, the secondary source identifies the equipment or surface from which the worker fell.              
10 May include some asphyxiations due to chest compressions.

13 Occupation data from 1992 to 2002 are based on the 1990 Occupational Classification System developed by the Bureau of the Census.              
14 Includes fatal injuries to persons identified as resident armed forces regardless of individual occupation listed.              
15 Industry data from 1992 to 2002 are classified according to the Standard Industrial Classification Manual, 1987.

17 Includes fatal injuries to workers employed by governmental organizations regardless of industry. Includes all fatal occupational injuries meeting this ownership              
criterion across all specified years, regardless of industry classification system.              
18 Includes all fatal occupational injuries meeting this ownership criterion across all specified years, regardless of industry classification system.
