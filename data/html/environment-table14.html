1. Cattle
These figures have been sourced from the Cattle Tracing System (CTS).  
The data includes returns from all holdings with cattle so are not subject to survey error. 
In CTS, the breed of the cattle is used to identify a breed purpose.  Around
round 2% of all female cattle do not have an assigned breed purpose or are of dual breed. 
 In all above results, these cattle have been allocated to either dairy or beef at CPH level
based on the other cattle on the holding.
Where there are no other cattle on the holding, they are allocated on the basis of the 
national split between dairy and beef in that age band.
2. Pigs
Other Sows - either being suckled or dry sows being kept for further breeding
Results for fattening pigs categories are often volatile as the "point in time" nature of the 
June survey can lead to large variations in the numbers in each weight band on 
holdings on survey day.
3. Poultry
The poultry population fluctuates significantly on a daily basis due to the production cycles.  
2007 figures broiler and broiler breeder figures nave been revised due to broilers being  
mis-classified as broiler breeders on several large poultry units. Approximately 770 
thousand poultry have been moved into the "Table chickens (broilers)" category, out of the 
Broiler breeders category.
